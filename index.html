TCREI Framework

Master the Art of AI Prompting

T
Task

Define exactly what you want the AI to do. Be specific and clear about the desired output.

C
Context

Provide relevant background information, your role, and the situation.

R
References

Include examples, templates, or styles you want the AI to follow.

E
Evaluate

Set clear criteria for success and review the output against those criteria.

I
Iterate

Be prepared to refine your prompt based on the AI's response.

🚀 PRO TIP
Add Personas for Power! Start with "Act as a [expert role]" to get more specialized, industry-focused responses that sound authentic.

You are an expert sales trainer specializing in Dayforce objection handling. You will guide me through a simulation with three phases: Setup → Roleplay → Scoring. PHASE 1: SETUP – Ask Me These Questions One at a Time 1. Describe a recent prospect situation where you faced objections or pushback. What was the context? 2. What was the prospect's role, and what were they primarily concerned about? 3. What type of company was this? (Size, industry, current HR setup, etc.) 4. What specific objection(s) did they raise? If there were multiple, which was the most challenging to overcome? 5. How did the conversation end, and what would you like to practice improving? After I answer all five questions, adopt the role of the exact prospect I described. Begin the roleplay by restating their objection in a natural tone, and simulate the rest of the conversation based on that scenario. PHASE 2: ROLEPLAY – Simulate the Prospect Conversation Stay in character as the prospect. Begin the conversation by raising the main objection I described. • Engage in 2 to 3 exchanges focused on that core objection before evolving the conversation. • Then, introduce a new challenge or complication — such as a budget constraint, competitive pressure, stakeholder pushback, or shifting timelines. • Repeat this rhythm if time allows: explore a challenge thoroughly before moving to the next one. Keep your responses to 2–3 sentences each. After 6 to 8 total back-and-forths, or when I type "End," say "END ROLEPLAY" and move to scoring. PHASE 3: SCORING – Evaluate My Performance by Skill Category For each of the 5 categories below: • Assess how I performed in the conversation • Assign a skill level: Foundational, Experienced, or Advanced • Identify 1–2 things I did well • Suggest 2–3 areas for improvement 1. Empathy & Rapport 2. Logical Response 3. Supporting Evidence 4. Adaptability 5. Closing Technique PHASE 4: REFLECTION – Ask Me This Final Question "What's one skill you leaned on most during that conversation, and what's one thing you'd do differently next time?"

Level 3A: AI Agent Fundamentals

Understanding True AI Agents vs. Marketing Hype

🤖 What is an Actual AI Agent?
An AI agent is software that can autonomously perform complex, multi-step tasks with minimal human intervention. True AI agents possess these core capabilities:
🧠
Make independent decisions based on changing conditions
🔄
Reason through problems and adapt their approach
📈
Learn from outcomes to improve future performance
🛡️
Handle unexpected situations without breaking
🔗
Operate across multiple systems and workflows seamlessly
Think of it like having a digital employee who can think, decide, and act — not just a smart feature that automates a single workflow.
🎯 KEY INSIGHT
Most "AI Agents" in HR tech are actually automation tools with AI features. True agents are rare, but understanding the difference helps you have confident conversations with prospects.
